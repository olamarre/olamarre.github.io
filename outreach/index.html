Outreach

I love sharing my passion for space exploration & robotics with students and the general public. Contrary to one might think, “rocket” science is accessible to anyone!

Being a conferencer, museum guide and camp counselor at Space Camp Canada for a few summers before starting university taught me the value of inspiring local and international communities to be excited about exploring worlds beyond our own. Our home, Earth, is literally a grain of sand drifting away in a vast ocean called the Universe – how can we not wonder about what’s out there?

Feel free to get in touch and/or engage with me on social media!

Some events I participated in

Since the beginning of my undergraduate studies, I took part in numerous public speaking events to increase the awareness on robotics and planetary exploration. A few of them were recorded and are available online:


Mars rover navigation 101: how robots safely drive on the red planet!

May 12, 2021
Similar event in French also available

Science Odyssey is Canada’s biggest science festival, bringing together science professionals and enthusiasts to raise the awareness on their field among the general public. I gave a brief introductory presentation about rover navigation on the Martian surface.

[French only] Perseverance Rover Landing Coverage with the Amateur Astronomers Association of Quebec

February 18, 2021

I had the honor to cover the Perseverance rover landing with the Amateur Astronomers Association of Quebec and give a talk about the rover’s navigation subsystems. Only available in French.

Three Minute Thesis (3MT) competition at the UofT Robotics Institute

November 12, 2020

I participated in the University of Toronto Robotics Institute’s 3MT competition, for which we have three minutes to summarize our academic research to a panel of judges not familiar with the field.

University Rover Challenge 2020 Alumni Panel

May 30, 2020

I participated in the 2020 University Rover Challenge (URC) alumni panel to give advice to the current URC participants and share some lessons that my team learned when we participated in the URC a few years before.

[French only] Space Robotics Q&A with the Cosmodôme

April 6, 2020

I had a live conversation about my projects with NASA’s Jet Propulsion Laboratory and the challenges of robotic planetary navigation with Marie-Michèle Limoges from the Cosmodôme / Space Camp Canada. Only available in French.

Talk at the 18th Annual International Mars Society Convention

September 17, 2015

Two of my colleagues and myself presented the first McGill Robotics Mars rover after competing at the 2015 University Rover Challenge and earning the third place at the 2015 European Rover Challenge. We also shared some lessons learned along the way.
